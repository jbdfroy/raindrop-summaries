    Title: Fuel Shortages in Russia Due to Ukrainian Drone Attacks on Oil Refineries

    In this article, we discuss the impact of Ukrainian drone attacks on Russian oil refineries and the effects these attacks have had on the Russian economy. Since August 2023, Ukraine has been targeting Russian oil refineries deep inside the country, causing fuel shortages for Russians. By October, Ukrainian drones had hit more than half of Russia’s 38 major refineries at least once, resulting in a decrease in processing oil from about 5.4 million barrels per day in July to roughly 5 million barrels per day in September.

    The Russian government has several tools to maintain a relative equilibrium and prevent a complete breakdown of the energy sector, including using its substantial surplus capacity as the world’s third-largest refining system and repairing damaged units quickly. However, the attacks on refineries could have far-reaching effects over the longer term as Russian resilience is gradually being worn out.

    The real vulnerability exposed by these attacks is Russia’s dependence on infrastructure built decades ago, which has been maintained through improvisation and preserved by command rather than innovation. The refineries are most likely to wear out under the weight of repeated shocks and institutional sclerosis, symbolizing Russia’s war economy itself.

    Ukraine cannot break Russia’s oil industry overnight, but its campaign ensures that Russia will have to pay an ever-higher cost to maintain stability. The refineries will keep operating, the pumps will keep running, and exports will continue, but with rising costs, shrinking margins, and a reduced capacity to recover from attack.
