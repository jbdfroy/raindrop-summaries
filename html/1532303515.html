    The article discusses the similarities between U.S. foreign policy under President Donald Trump and the period of U.S. expansionism in the late 19th century, specifically after the Spanish-American War in 1898. It highlights how both eras have a focus on economic power, territorial acquisitions, and a belief in the superiority of Western civilization. The author warns that Trump’s interventionist policies in Venezuela, driven by these same principles, could lead to unintended consequences, as was the case with the U.S. annexation of the Philippines in 1898, which resulted in a long and costly insurgency. The article suggests that Trump should be cautious about becoming too deeply involved in Venezuela, as it may create problems that are difficult to extract from.

    Keywords: U.S. foreign policy, interventionism, expansionism, economic power, territorial acquisitions, civilization, Venezuela, Philippines, insurgency, caution, unintended consequences.
