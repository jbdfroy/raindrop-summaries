Titre : ‘The Decline of Post-1945 Order Under Trump’s Anarchy: A New Form of Hobbesian World’

‘Foreign Affairs’ (January 2023)

Article :

In the post-World War II era, the United States and its allies established an order that promoted peace among great powers, open trade, and international cooperation. However, under President Donald Trump’s leadership, this order is declining rapidly. This emerging anarchy is characterized by a lack of restraint, unpredictability, and a disregard for both domestic and international rules, bringing about a world similar to Thomas Hobbes’ vision of “all against all.”

Anarchy in International Relations

In the realm of international relations, realist scholars argue that anarchy is the absence of any authority enforcing global rules. Despite this, anarchy does not necessarily equate to chaos. Realists believe that the lack of a central authority can function as a constraint, forcing states to act prudently and husband their resources. The risk of war often causes even great powers to think twice about aggressive actions.

Hegemonic Stability Theory

One prominent theory suggests that international stability is more likely when one country dominates, providing public goods such as monetary institutions or security alliances, creating and enforcing rules, and facilitating economic exchange and cooperation. However, this hegemonic order eventually falls due to overextension and the rise of new powers challenging for global dominance.

Trump’s Anarchic Approach

Since his return to office, Trump has accelerated the decline of the post-1945 order. He disregards domestic institutions and international norms, embracing a more chaotic anarchy closer to Hobbes’ vision of “nasty, brutish, and short.” In this world, anything goes, and order may eventually emerge, but it is unlikely to be led or benefited by the United States.

Implications for U.S. Hegemony

Trump’s actions have caused critical decisions with little process at seemingly random times, acting aggressively in multiple regions at once. His unpredictable impulses and quick decision-making represent a new level of chaos that no previous great power could contemplate. This new form of anarchy is different because a dominant power with democratic institutions for centuries is quickly unwinding them, creating a world where “not only would the rules be bent, but the rules themselves would disappear.”

Conclusion

In Trump’s anarchic world view, the United States is acting with few constraints, both domestically and internationally. This Hobbesian anarchy will have long-lasting consequences for U.S. power and stability in the international system. Whether this approach succeeds or not, it weakens the United States and makes China relatively more attractive to smaller powers seeking security.
